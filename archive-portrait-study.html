Text as Image: Portrait Study

An experimental typographic portrait study exploring how repeated text, density, and direction can construct image, mood, and narrative. The piece treats typography as both language and texture, using text flow to shape facial structure, shadow, and emotional tone.

Experimental Typography  ·  Visual Composition  ·  Type System  ·  2025

What I Learned

This study helped me understand typography as both language and image. By experimenting with density, direction, repetition, and contrast, I learned how text can build form, shadow, and emotional tone while still maintaining visual rhythm and compositional control.

The hardest part was keeping the portrait legible as a face while pushing the type dense enough to read as texture — too sparse and the likeness disappeared, too dense and it became noise. Finding that threshold sharpened how I think about information density more generally: the same tension shows up in interface work, where a screen has to read clearly at a glance while still holding enough detail to be useful up close.
